# Tier-B Possession / Eviction Notice Periods — Sourced (all non-banned countries beyond Tier A)

**Compiled:** 2026-06-25 by Claude Code from public/official + reputable legal sources.
**Status:** `researched` — citable, Propvora-drafted, **informational only**. Every jurisdiction shows a **permanent** "not legal advice — verify with a qualified local professional & customise" disclaimer (see `LIABILITY-disclaimer-and-customizability-plan.md`).
**Companion:** Tier-A (`tier-a-notice-periods-sourced.md`) holds EW/SCT/NI/IE in deeper detail.
**Operator override (founder directive):** every figure below is a **default**. The wizard must let the operator **override the notice period** when a contractual term, statutory exemption, or rule-changing factor applies — captured with a reason/exemption note + audit entry (design in master plan §11).

> **Honesty rails:** (1) **Federal countries (AU, CA, US) have NO single national notice period** — they are state/province statutes; representative figures are labelled as such. (2) Many countries require **court/tribunal eviction** even after notice — notice ≠ self-help eviction. (3) Figures are landlord→tenant residential, indefinite/standard tenancy unless noted.

## Cross-cutting notes for encoding
1. **Region key required** for AU/CA/US (state/province) and GB (EW/SCT/NI) — the framework pack is keyed `countryCode[:region]`.
2. **Court/tribunal step** is near-universal — the framework records the competent body (RDC, Tanfeedh, FtT, LTB, Sulh Hukuk, etc.) and states "notice ≠ eviction; court order required".
3. **Override/exemption** (founder directive): the operator can change the default notice period per case with a reason/exemption note; the override is audited and the draft shows "operator-overridden (default was X)".
4. **Provenance:** all rows here = `researched`. None show the "Reviewed ✓" badge until counsel sign-off per `jurisdiction_review_signoff`.
